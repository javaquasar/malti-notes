The two key patterns on this page are Huwa kien / Hija kienet and Huwa għamel / Hija għamlet.

Section 1

Core Idea

Se naqraw dwar xi personaġġi ta' impatt minn madwar id-dinja u minn Malta.
We are going to read about some impactful personalities from around the world and from Malta.
F'din it-tema huwa importanti li naraw kif nużaw il-verb kien jew kienet.
In this topic it is important to see how we use the verb kien or kienet.
Nużaw ukoll għamel jew għamlet biex nitkellmu dwar bidla, xogħol, jew impatt.
We also use għamel or għamlet to talk about change, work, or impact.

Section 5

Biography Sentence Pattern

Isem: ...Name of the person
Huwa kien ... / Hija kienet ...Job, role, or adjective
Huwa għamel ... / Hija għamlet ...What the person did
Huwa / Hija importanti għax ...Why the person matters

Ready-made mini biography

Hija kienet attivista.
She was an activist.
Hija għamlet impatt kbir.
She made a big impact.
Hija importanti għax ġġieldet għal edukazzjoni aħjar.
She is important because she fought for better education.

Section 11

Culture Note

Oliver Friggieri and Il-Bikja tal-Buskett

Fil-klassi tkellimna wkoll fuq Oliver Friggieri.
In class we also spoke about Oliver Friggieri.
Huwa kien poeta, filosfu, u professur Malti.
He was a Maltese poet, philosopher, and professor.
Fil-poeżija Il-Bikja tal-Buskett naraw kliem dwar in-natura, il-ħarruba, u l-ambjent.
In the poem Il-Bikja tal-Buskett we see language about nature, the carob tree, and the environment.
Section 12

Common Mistakes

Do not mix up kien and kienetHuwa kien for a man, but Hija kienet for a woman.
Do not mix up għamel and għamletHuwa għamel for a man, but Hija għamlet for a woman.
Watch masculine and feminine jobsavukat / avukata, kantant / kantanta, għalliem / għalliema.
Watch masculine and feminine adjectivesfamuż / famuża, onest / onesta, kuraġġuż / kuraġġuża.
