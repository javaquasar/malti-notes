The most important idea is simple: the collective often talks about a group or quantity, but adjectives and verbs usually stay in the singular.

Section 1

Core Idea

What the collective shows

Il-kollettiv fil-Malti juri ġabra ta' oġġetti tal-istess għamla, kwalità jew tip.
The collective in Maltese shows a collection of objects of the same form, quality, or type.
Għalkemm juri sens ta' kwantità, ħafna drabi jieħu aġġettiv u verb fis-singular.
Even though it shows quantity, it often takes an adjective and a verb in the singular.

Section 2

Main Patterns

Singular one item tuffieħa one apple
Collective fruit / food / insects in general or in quantity tuffieħ apples as a group or quantity
Counted Form when the lesson gives a number + plural form żewġ basliet two onions
Specific Number use the plural when counting individual items żewġ larinġiet two oranges
Large Quantity use the collective with much, some, kilo, half kilo ħafna larinġ a lot of oranges
Adjective Rule the collective usually takes the masculine singular adjective tuffieħ aħdar green apples / apple fruit

Ready-made shopping lines

Agħmel lista ta' frott u ħaxix li tħobb tixtri.
Make a list of fruit and vegetables that you like to buy.
Pereżempju: kilo brunġiel, wiżna patata, żewġ basliet, larinġ, lumi.
For example: a kilo of aubergines, a sack of potatoes, two onions, oranges, lemons.
Għandna bżonn tuffieħ.
We need apples.
Għandi bżonn larinġ.
I need oranges.

Section 10

Common Mistakes

Do not keep the feminine adjective with the collective tuffieħa ħamra is singular, but tuffieħ aħmar is collective.
Do not use the collective after a counted number if the lesson gives a plural form Say żewġ basliet, not just a bare collective after the number.
The collective is not always the same as the singular tadama -> tadam, nemusa -> nemus, ħuta -> ħut.
Use the collective naturally in shopping language With ħafna, ftit, naqra, kilo, and nofs kilo, the lesson usually uses the collective.
