Core Patterns

How the Course Builds Verb Sentences

Simple Present

subject + present verb
  • Jien niekol. = I eat.
  • Hija tixtri. = She buys.
  • Huma jimxu. = They walk.

Present Continuous

subject + qed / qiegħed / qiegħda / qegħdin + verb
  • Il-mara qiegħda tixtri. = The woman is shopping.
  • Ir-raġel qiegħed jiekol. = The man is eating.
  • Huma qegħdin jimxu. = They are walking.

Past

subject + past form
  • Ilbieraħ mort il-ħanut. = Yesterday I went to the shop.
  • Huwa kien famuż. = He was famous.
  • Hija għamlet impatt. = She made an impact.

Future

subject + se + verb
  • Jien se niekol. = I will eat.
  • Aħna se mmorru. = We will go.
  • Huma se jixorbu. = They will drink.

"To Be" in Present Identity Sentences

In the present tense Maltese often omits an explicit verb "to be" in identity sentences. The gender difference usually appears in the noun or adjective, not in a separate present-tense verb.
Pronoun Neutral Identity Example Gender Example / Note
Jien / Jiena Jien student u għandi tletin sena. Jien Malti. / Jiena Maltija.
Int / Inti Int student u għandek tletin sena. Int Malti. / Int Maltija.
Hu Hu student u għandu tletin sena. Hu Malti.

  • Jien student. = I am a student.
  • Hi studenta. = She is a student.
  • Hu tabib. = He is a doctor.
  • Hi tabiba. = She is a doctor.
  • Jien student u għandi tletin sena. = I am a student and I am thirty years old.
  • Hi studenta u għandha tletin sena. = She is a student and she is thirty years old.

Root vs Stem

Semitic Verbs: Root System

Source-based idea from the lesson: many Maltese verbs of Arabic/Semitic origin are built from a root l-għerq.

qagħad -> q-għ-dto stay / to live
ħabb -> ħ-B-Bto like / to love
lagħabto play
ħademto work
rebahto win
raqadto sleep
qarato read
ġerato run

Borrowed Verbs: Stem

Source-based idea from the lesson: verbs from Italian and ènglish are explained through a stem zokk morfemiku rather than a Semitic root.

niskennja -> skennscan
nipprintja -> printprint
niffirma -> firmasign
nimmarka -> markmark
niċċekkja -> ċekkcheck
naċċerta -> ċertmake sure
Semitic verb -> root    Italian / English verb -> stem

This distinction is one of the most useful takeaways from Lesson 6, because it helps explain why different verbs conjugate differently.

Lesson 6

Plural Endings: -u vs -aw

Common Semitic Pattern

Source-based summary from the lesson: plural forms of many Semitic verbs often end in -u.

  • Aħna nħobbu / Intom tħobbu / Huma jħobbu
  • Aħna noqogħdu / Intom toqogħdu / Huma joqogħdu
  • Aħna nieklu / Intom tieklu / Huma jieklu

Common Borrowed Pattern

Source-based summary from the lesson: plural forms of many Italian / English verbs often end in -aw.

  • Aħna nittrejnjaw / Intom tittrejnjaw / Huma jittrejnjaw
  • Aħna niddisinjaw / Intom tiddisinjaw / Huma jiddisinjaw
  • Aħna nikkollezzjonaw / Intom tikkollezzjonaw / Huma jikkollezzjonaw
  • Aħna nipprintjaw / Intom tipprintjaw / Huma jipprintjaw
many Semitic plurals -> -u    many borrowed plurals -> -aw

Useful warning: this is a strong study pattern from the lesson, but not a rule for every verb without exception.

Lesson 6

Xi tħobb tagħmel? Pattern

Core Formula

ħabb in present + another verb in the imperfect / present-type form
  • Inħobb naħdem. = I like to work.
  • Inħobb niekol. = I like to eat.
  • Inħobb nixrob. = I like to drink.
  • Inħobb nimxi. = I like to walk.

By Person

  • Jien inħobb ...
  • Int tħobb ...
  • Huwa jħobb ...
  • Hi tħobb ...
  • Aħna nħobbu ...
  • Intom tħobbu ...
  • Huma jħobbu ...

Useful Lesson 6 Sentence Models

  • Jien inħobb nagħmel kejk. = I like to make a cake.
  • Inti tħobb tagħmel l-isports. = You like to do sports.
  • Huwa jħobb jagħmel ix-xogħol tad-dar. = He likes to do housework.
  • Hi tħobb tagħmel il-ħbieb. = She likes to make friends.
  • Aħna nħobbu nagħmlu x-xogħol fil-ġnien. = We like to do work in the garden.

This is one of the most useful speaking patterns in Level 1 for homework, self-description, and conversation practice.

Lesson 6

Inserted i Note

What the Lesson Says

  • nħobb is often heard like inħobb.
  • The lesson note says the vowel i is only added when needed.
  • That means pronunciation can help the word sound easier, even if the written verb focus stays on the consonant pattern.

Safe Way To Remember It

  • Jiena nħobb is the safe written form to keep in your answers.
  • If you hear something closer to inħobb, do not panic: the lesson explains that this extra vowel can appear for pronunciation.
  • For homework, keep the standard written form from the tables.

This is a pronunciation and reading note from Lesson 6, useful when spoken Maltese sounds slightly fuller than the written consonant pattern.

Table Form vs Real-Life Form

Many learners get confused because the neat form in the table and the form they hear in real speech do not always sound identical at the beginning.

Table Form Real-Life Form You May Hear Meaning Why It Happens
nħobb inħobb I like / I love A helping vowel can appear before the form to make pronunciation easier.
nħallas inħallas I pay The grammar stays first person singular even if the spoken start sounds fuller.
nżur inżur I visit The spoken form keeps the same verb; it only sounds smoother.
nġemma’ inġemma’ I save up The extra i is not a new tense or separate word.
nħoss inħoss I feel You should still connect it to the same table pattern when studying.
nħossni inħossni I feel / I feel myself The same helping vowel can appear even when the course form already includes an attached object ending.
nsuq insuq I drive Spoken Maltese may add the vowel for easier pronunciation at the start of the word.

How To Study These Forms

  • First memorise the clean table form, for example nħobb.
  • Then learn the real-life spoken partner, for example inħobb.
  • Do not treat the extra i as a separate particle, prefix with a new meaning, or a different tense.
  • Use the local verb dialog on this page to connect the heard form back to the full verb table.

Imperative

How To Use the Imperative

The imperative is used for commands, instructions, and direct requests. In practice, Level 1 students usually need the forms for Int and Intom.

Main Idea

singular imperative - one person    plural imperative - more than one person
  • Ejja! = Come!
  • Mur! = Go!
  • Kul! = Eat!
  • Ixrob! = Drink!
  • Ara! = Look!
  • Isma'! = Listen!

Plural Forms

  • Morru! = Go!
  • Klu! = Eat!
  • Ixorbu! = Drink!
  • Aqraw! = Read!
  • IsimgHu! = Listen!
  • Ejja, ejjew! = come / come all together [basic classroom use]

Useful Everyday Commands

Maltese English Use
Ejja hawn. Come here. calling one person
Mur id-dar. Go home. simple direction
Kul il-ħobż. Eat the bread. food context
Ixrob l-ilma. Drink the water. food / health context
Aqra l-ktieb. Read the book. classroom context
Ikteb ismek. Write your name. classroom / form filling
Agħlaq il-bieb. Close the door. home context
Iftaħ it-tieqa. Open the window. home context

Polite Requests

A very safe way to make the imperative softer is to add Jekk jogħġbok.

  • Jekk jogħġbok, agħlaq il-bieb. = Please close the door.
  • Jekk jogħġbok, oqgħod bilqiegħda. = Please sit down.
  • Jekk jogħġbok, ikteb ismek. = Please write your name.

Important Note

  • Imperative forms are very useful, but they do not all follow one simple rule.
  • It is best to learn the most common forms as ready-made chunks.
  • For less common verbs, use the local verb finder on this page to open the forms table.

For Level 1 speaking and classroom use, the most useful imperative verbs are the practical ones: come, go, eat, drink, read, write, open, close, listen, and look.

  • Ta' min hu l-flokk? -> Hu l-flokk tiegħu.
  • Min huma? -> Huma l-ġenituri tiegħi.

This block is useful when you need to answer quickly about family members, objects in a picture, and ownership.

Answer Chains

How To Build Longer Answers

From 1 Sentence to 2

main answer + one detail
  • Jien student. -> Jien student. Noqgħod Malta.
  • Hi oħti. -> Hi oħti. Hija ferħana.
  • Hemm familja. -> Hemm familja. Huma fil-kċina.
  • Inħobb il-frott. -> Inħobb il-frott. Inħobb ukoll il-ħaxix.

From 2 Sentences to 3

who / where / what doing
  • Hemm missier. Huwa fil-kċina. Qiegħed isajjar.
  • Hi ommi. Hija d-dar. Qiegħda tixrob te.
  • Hemm żewġt itfal. Huma fil-park. Qegħdin jilagħbu.
  • Din id-dar tiegħi. Hija kbira. Għandha ġnien żgħir.

From 3 Sentences to 4

who + where + action + opinion/detail
  • Hemm familja. Huma fil-kċina. Qegħdin jieklu. Huma ferħanin.
  • Hemm mara. Hija fil-ħanut. Qiegħda tixtri. Għandha borża bajda.
  • Hemm raġel. Huwa fil-park. Qiegħed jimxi. Il-post huwa sabiħ.
  • Din oħti. Hija d-dar. Qiegħda tistudja. Hija kalma.

Safe Connectors

  • u = and
  • wkoll = also
  • imma = but
  • għax = because
  • imbagħad = then [basic use]
  • Jien inħobb il-kafè u t-te.
  • Hi ommi u hija d-dar.
  • Inħobb il-frott, imma ma nħobbx il-laħam.
  • Huwa importanti għax jgħin ħafna.

Picture Answer Chains

  • Fl-istampa hemm familja. Huma d-dar. Qegħdin fil-kċina. Qegħdin jieklu.
  • Fl-istampa hemm tifel. Huwa fil-park. Qiegħed jilgħab. Huwa ferħan.
  • Fl-istampa hemm mara u raġel. Huma fil-ħanut. Qegħdin jitkellmu. Huma qed jixtru.

Homework Answer Chains

  • Għandi familja żgħira. Għandi omm u missier. Aħna noqogħdu f'dar. Id-dar tagħna hija kbira.
  • Inħobb il-frott. Inħobb ukoll il-ħobż u l-ġobon. Filgħodu nieħu kafè. Filgħaxija nixrob te.
  • Jien student. Noqgħod Malta. Inħobb nistudja. Ma nħobbx inqum kmieni.

Use these chains in oral tasks when one short answer is not enough. Start small and only add one safe detail at a time.
