Lesson 4.1 Demonstratives

Demonstrative Pronouns

Near: this / these

Form Use Slide Example
dan masculine singular, near Dan sufan. = This is a sofa.
din feminine singular, near Din mejda. = This is a table.
dawn plural, near Dawn pjanti. = These are plants.

Far: that / those

Form Use Slide Example
dak masculine singular, far Dak sufan. = That is a sofa.
dik feminine singular, far Dik mejda. = That is a table.
dawk plural, far Dawk pjanti. = Those are plants.
near: dan / din / dawn    far: dak / dik / dawk

Main Idea

Gender matters.Use dan / dak with masculine nouns and din / dik with feminine nouns.
Plural is easier.Use dawn for near plural and dawk for far plural.
Distance matters.If the thing is close or within reach, use dan / din / dawn. If it is far, use dak / dik / dawk.
In this lesson they usually come before the noun.Dan sufan, Dik tieqa, Dawn pjanti.

Quick ċhoice by Noun

is-saqafmasculine -> dan / dak
tieqafeminine -> din / dik
bozzafeminine -> din / dik
purtierafeminine -> din / dik
il-kuxinsplural -> dawn / dawk

Source-Based Practice Answers

Din bozza.This is a bulb.
Dan is-saqaf.This is the ceiling.
Dik tieqa.That is a window.
Dawn il-kuxins.These are the cushions.
Din purtiera.This is a curtain.

Added-éxample ċlarifiers

Dan ktieb.Use dan because ktieb is masculine.
Din karozza.Use din because karozza is feminine.
Dawk siġġijiet.Use dawk because the noun is plural and far.

Lesson 4 Particle

The Particle minn + Article

minn + article -> the form changes and often becomes shorter before the noun

Source-Based Pattern Table

Pattern Result Slide Example
minn + il- / l- mill- / mil- mill-Estonja, mill-Polonja, mil-Libja
minn + iċ- miċ- miċ-Ċina
minn + id- mid- mid-Danimarka
minn + in- min- min-Niġerja
minn + ir- mir- mir-Russja
minn + is- mis- mis-Serbja
minn + it- mit- mit-Turkija
minn + ix- mix- mix-Xemxija
minn + iż- miż- miż-Żimbabwe
minn + iż- miż- miż- [as shown on the slide]

Main Idea

minn gets shorter.Before article forms, minn usually contracts and keeps only the sound that matches the next article form.
The first consonant matters.You do not keep full minn; instead you get forms like mill-, mid-, mir-, mis-.
This is a spelling and pronunciation pattern.It helps Maltese sound smooth when saying where somebody or something comes from.
Use the learned form as one chunk.For Level 1 it is safer to memorise mill-Polonja or mir-Russja as whole units.

How To Use It

Jien minn Malta.Basic full form without article contraction.
Jien mill-Estonja.Use the contracted form from the slide.
ħu mir-Russja.The minn form changes to match ir-.
ħija mit-Turkija.The minn form changes to match it-.

What To Notice

Source-based:mill-Estonja, miċ-Ċina, mid-Danimarka, mir-Russja, mis-Serbja, mit-Turkija, mix-Xemxija, miż-Żimbabwe.
Added-example:Jien mill-Estonja., ħu mir-Russja., ħija mit-Turkija.

These small words are very important in picture description, family answers, and home or food homework.

Quick Answers

Ready-Made Short Answers

Question -> Answer

Min hi? -> Hi oħti.Who is she? -> She is my sister.
Min hu? -> Hu missieri.Who is he? -> He is my father.
Ta' min hi d-dar? -> Hi d-dar tiegħi.Whose house is it’ -> It is my house.
Ta' min hu l-flokk? -> Hu l-flokk tiegħu.Whose shirt is it’ -> It is his shirt.
Ta' min huma t-tfal? -> Huma t-tfal tagħhom.Whose children are they? -> They are their children.

Safe Mini Drill

who? / whose? + short answer + one detail
Hi oħti. Hija ferħana.She is my sister. She is happy.
Hu missieri. Huwa d-dar.He is my father. He is at home.
Hi d-dar tagħna. Hija kbira.It is our house. It is big.
Huma t-tfal tagħhom. Huma fil-park.They are their children. They are in the park.
